Origin of Bonsai Plants

Bonsai is a Japanese art form that involves growing miniature trees in containers. The practice of bonsai is said to have started over a thousand years ago in China, and was later introduced to Japan. In Japan, bonsai became highly stylized and was developed into the sophisticated art form that it is today. Bonsai gained popularity in the West in the later 19th and early 20th centuries, and is now enjoyed by people all over the world. The art of bonsai involves careful pruning and training of the tree to achieve a desired shape, as well as judicious use of techniques such as wiring, potting, and fertilizing. Today, bonsai is considered both a form of artistic expression, a reflection of nature in miniature and a horticultural practice which is enjoyed by millions of people.
